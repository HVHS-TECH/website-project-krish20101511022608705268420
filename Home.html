Mitsubishi Lancer Evolutions

Information on Mitsubishi Lancer Evolutions History

The Mitsbishi Lancer Evolution, commonly referred to as the "Evo," is a high performance Trim of the Mitsubishi Lancer. The Evo was produced by Mitsubishi Motors from 1992 to 2016, an it is known for its its engine, the 4G63T. The 4G63T is a 2.0L Turbocharged inline 4 engine that produces around 202kw of power. The Evo was designed to be a rally car, and it was used in various rally competitions around the world. The Evo was also popular among car enthusiasts for its performance and handling capabilities, and it has a strong reputation in the automotive community. The Evo was prouced in serval different versions, each with its own unique features and specifications. The Evo is also known for its distinctive stlying, which includes a large rear wing and aggressive body kit. The Evo has a strong following among car enthusiasts, and it is considered to be one of the most iconic performance carsof its era. (1992 to 1994) The Evo I was created to homologate the Group A rally parts, using the 2.0L 4G63T engine and a AWD(All wheel Drive) system. then in 1994 When the evo II came into the rally it offered better handling. (1995 to 1996) The Evo III delivered Mitsubishi's first out right WRC win, while in 1996 when the Evo IV came in rally, It came with Active YAW control system. (1996 to 1999) A Finnish driver Tommi Mäkinen won 4 WRC championships in a row with the Evo III, IV, V and VI, which made the Evo very popular again. (1998) The Evo V helped Mitsubishi secure the 1998 WRC Condtructors championship, even while competing against newer WRC rally cars with a group A car. (2001 to 2005) The Evo VII and the Evo VIII continuted to compete but they teams competivness faded away. Leading to Mitsubishi pulling out of the WRC in 2005.
